# Department heads — overview

As a **department head**, you connect your department’s **plans** (curriculum) to **real courses and activities** each term, and you make sure **students** and **instructors** are attached to the right offerings.

Everything you manage is limited to the **department(s) you head**. Other departments in the same **college** have their own data.

---

## What you are responsible for (in plain language)

| Area | What it means for you |
|------|------------------------|
| **Curriculum courses** | Templates or catalog entries your department uses to plan teaching. |
| **Curriculum activities** | Types of activities (for example, practicum, lecture block) your department uses. |
| **Courses** | Actual course offerings for a term, linked to your department. |
| **Activities** | Scheduled activities inside a course, with dates and rules. |
| **Activity weeks** | Weeks inside an activity where students report sessions and surveys apply. |
| **Students** | Learners you attach to courses and activities through **enrollments**. |
| **Users** | Staff accounts tied to your department when your process includes them. |

Your college may use slightly different words in training—if unsure, match the **menu names** you see on screen.

---

## Typical workflow (high level)

1. Make sure **curriculum** items exist or are updated for your department (sometimes a **quality lead** helps at the **college** level).
2. Create or open **courses** for the current period.
3. Add **activities** to those courses and configure **weeks** and expectations (sessions, hours—your **university’s** rules).
4. **Enroll students** and assign **instructors** where required.
5. During the term, adjust only what your **college** allows (some items **lock** after a course or activity has **passed**).

## Getting help

- **College administrator:** new staff accounts, roles, college-wide settings (`admin` on the college subdomain).
- **Education quality lead:** survey wording, statements, checkpoints, college-level curriculum catalog where the role allows.
- **Platform support:** only when your administrator confirms a technical problem with the whole site.
