# Students and enrollments (department heads)

Students **only see** courses and activities they are **enrolled** in. Enrolling is one of the most important steps before the term.

---

## Student records

Depending on your college, students may be:

- **Created** by a department head or administrator, or  
- **Imported** in bulk, or  
- **Self-registered** on the student portal (if enabled).

You can usually **search** students by name or ID where your permissions allow.

## Course enrollment

To attach a student to a **course**:

1. Open the **course** (or the student profile—use whichever screen your college trained you on).
2. Find **enrollments** or **enrolled students**.
3. **Add** the student to the course.
4. Save and confirm the student appears in the list.

## Activity enrollment

Many reporting tasks (class sessions, surveys) depend on **activity enrollment**, not only course enrollment.

1. Open the **activity**.
2. Find **enrollments** or **students** for that activity.
3. Add students who should report for that activity.
4. If your process uses **informant** or special roles, set them according to your department’s rules.

## Instructors on an activity

If your **university** assigns an **instructor** to each activity:

1. Open the **activity**.
2. Use **assign instructor** (or similar).
3. Choose the staff member and save.

Students and feedback flows may use this link to show the right person **performance** or comments.

## Tips to avoid common problems

- **Double-check the department** before enrolling—your view is scoped to departments you head, but habits from paper forms can still cause mistakes.
- If a student says “I see nothing,” verify **both** course and **activity** enrollment and that the **activity week** is open.
- Prefer **adding** enrollments early; last-minute changes can confuse students mid-week.

---

## Privacy reminder

Student data is sensitive. Only use it for **official** academic purposes. If someone asks for a full export, follow your **university’s** **data protection** rules.
