# Courses, activities, and weeks (department heads)

This page describes the **main building blocks** you work with. Exact field names on your screen may vary slightly by **college** configuration.

---

## Curriculum vs live courses

- **Curriculum courses / activities** are often prepared **once** and reused as a **pattern**.
- **Courses** and **activities** under your department are the **live** records for a specific run (term, cohort, dates).

Think of curriculum as the **blueprint** and courses as the **building** for that year.

---

## Creating or updating a course

1. Open your **department** in APD.
2. Go to **courses** (or the equivalent menu your college uses).
3. **Add** a course or open an existing one.
4. Fill in **dates**, **level**, **semester**, and links to **curriculum** if the form asks for them.
5. Save and confirm the course appears in your department list.

**After a course has passed**, some edits may be **disabled** to protect historical records.

---

## Activities inside a course

1. Open the **course**.
2. Find **activities** for that course.
3. Add an **activity** with the correct **type**, **dates**, and **instructor** if your process uses one.
4. Save.

Activities often drive which **students** see reporting tasks once they are **enrolled**.

---

## Activity weeks

**Activity weeks** divide an activity into **reporting periods** (for example, one week at a time).

Typical steps:

1. Open the **activity**.
2. Open **weeks** (or “activity weeks”).
3. Add or adjust **week names**, **start and end dates**, **expected sessions or hours**, and whether a week is **editable** by students—according to your **college’s** policy.
4. Save.

Students usually see **open** weeks on their **student portal** when they are enrolled.

## Imports and bulk tools

If your college uses **import** from spreadsheets for curriculum or statements, follow the **on-screen instructions** and templates your administrator provides. When in doubt, run a **small test** import first.

---

## Checklist before the term starts

- [ ] Courses exist with correct **dates** and **status**.
- [ ] Each running activity has **weeks** covering the teaching period.
- [ ] **Students** are enrolled (see [Students and enrollments](students-and-enrollments)).
- [ ] **Instructors** are assigned where required.
