# Surveys, checkpoints, and statements (education quality lead)

Use this page as a **roadmap**. Your college may use different menu labels—align with what you see on screen. Routes are listed in [application capabilities](../1-introduction/application-capabilities).

---

## Surveys

1. Open **surveys** from your staff navigation.
2. **Create** or **duplicate** a survey to start from a known-good template.
3. Edit **title**, **introduction**, and **sections** as your committee approves.
4. Link the survey to the **courses** or processes your **university** defines (follow local training).
5. **Preview** as a staff user before students see changes.

## Statements (qualitative)

**Statements** are the building blocks for qualitative analysis screens.

1. Open **statements** (or qualitative analysis) from the navigation.
2. **Add** a statement with clear wording **non-specialists** can understand.
3. Maintain **options** where your model uses fixed answers.
4. Use **import** only with approved spreadsheets and after backup / test import.

## Checkpoints

**Checkpoints** mark stages in review or data collection.

1. Open **checkpoints**.
2. **Create** or reorder checkpoints to match your accreditation or internal timeline.
3. Confirm **department heads** know when each checkpoint affects reporting.

Checkpoints list with order or status.  
File: `docs/public/screenshots/guide-eql-checkpoints-list.png`
:::

## Curriculum catalog (college scope)

When your role includes **curriculum courses** or **activity types** at **college** scope (see `Ability`):

1. Work from the **catalog** menus (not individual department course copies).
2. Coordinate renames with **department heads** so running courses stay aligned.
3. Document changes in your **university’s** **change log** if you maintain one.
